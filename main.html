Projects

Community Builder

I lead and engage with multiple organizations to support the growth of my fellow peers and related institutions. As Vice President of the University of Surrey's Computing Society, my efforts focused on delivering career development opportunities, increasing industrial awareness and engagement, and importantly supporting the progession of women in STEM.

As an advocate for women in STEM, I led and organized the first Women in STEM panel and networking event in partnership with other Surrey STEM-based societies.

Defying the Odds: Women in STEM Panel

This panel was open to everyone from all degrees to expose the challenges of being a woman in STEM and offer advice for achieving success within the STEM industries. The panel featured an international array of leading women whos roles ranged from engineer to Vice President from companies like Amazon, Goldman Sachs, and Intecsea to offer a collection of perspectives.

Goldman Sach's Code Your Escape

After obtaining the Computing Society's first sponsorship with Goldman Sachs, I organized a Code Your Escape challenge with Goldman Sachs engineers assisting the cross-degree teams.

Contracting 101: How to be a Contractor

I invited in an expert contractor, whos previous experience includes working at Microsoft, BMW, and Sky, to introduce and explain to people across all degrees how to best navigate a career as a contractor and it's various pro's and con's.

Computing Open Day Assistant

As a representative of the Computer Science department, I discussed the course with prospective students and parents. I regularly delivered presentations to all guests about settling into univeristy and the industrial placement year.

International Student Ambassador

I wrote a series of blogs from the perspective of an American studying in England at Surrey to offer advice and insight into life at Surrey. I often gave personal campus tours to prospective students and helped the students adapt to their new home once they had moved.

Computing Course Representative

Over four years, I actively championed the development and improvement of the department and wider university based on student feedback and personal observations. I worked collaboratively with the department to ensure a constructive solution was identified and implemented.

A Recent Graduate

... from the University of Surrey with a BSc(Hons) degree in Computing and Information technology where I learnt primarily computer science topics supplemented with business management and entrepreneurship modules. I was honored to be awarded the "IBM Prize for Best Final Year Performance".

As a part of my studies, I completed an industrial placement year at Microsoft as a software engineer working on Paint 3D in London. This led to the opportunity to work on the Xbox team at Microsoft's HQ in Redmond, WA for a period of three months.
