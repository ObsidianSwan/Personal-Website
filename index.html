Community Efforts

I actively engage with multiple organizations - University of Surrey, DigiGirlz, and Code First: Girls - to support the growth of my fellow peers and related institutions. My motivation is to empower others in finding their success whether it be my fellow Surrey students or by advocating for women in STEM. My efforts focus on delivering career development opportunities, mentorship, and increasing student industrial awareness and engagement.

Defying the Odds: Women in STEM Panel

As VP of the Computing Society, I organized a panel discussion about the challenges of being a woman in STEM and to provide advice for achieving success within the STEM industries. The panel offered a collection of perspectives from an international array of women whos roles ranged from engineer to Vice President from companies like Amazon, Goldman Sachs, and Intecsea

DigiGirlz Volunteer

Attended by over 150+ year 8 girls from a variety of local UK schools, I assisted the girls during the brainstorming and implementation phases of an accessibility hackathon, and provided presentation feedback and advice in anticipation of their project judging by Cindy Rose, the CEO of Microsoft UK.

Introduced Scratch Jr into a Montessori Preschool

In an effort to counter the concept that STEM is "just for boys", I introduced Scratch JR into a local Montessori preschool's curriculum. The feedback from both the girls and parents was incredibly positive with one girl remarking the programming was her favorite bit!

Computing Course Representative

Over four years, I actively championed the development and improvement of the department and wider university based on student feedback and personal observations. I worked collaboratively with the department to ensure a constructive solution was identified and implemented. For my efforts, I was awarded the Gold Course Representative Award.
